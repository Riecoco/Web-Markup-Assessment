Dear Younger Me,

Written by Nana Yaa Osei-Amoako

August 14, 2024

As I reflect on my freshman year, I realize that I've learned so much along the way. If I could give some advice to my younger self, here are the top five tips that I would share, hoping that they will help you make the most of your first year:

1. Start Projects Sooner

Procrastination might feel easy in the moment, but it can significantly affect your grades. Starting projects early gives you the time to refine your work, ask questions, and make improvements. Whether it's a design project or coding assignment, the earlier you start, the more room you have for feedback and revisions. Trust me, those extra days of polish can make a huge difference.

2. Ask for Help—A Lot!

I know it might feel intimidating to ask questions or seek help, but don't let that stop you. Whether it's reaching out to a teacher for clarification or emailing for feedback, it's crucial to stay proactive. Also, get to know your classmates. Socializing makes it easier to ask for help when you need it—sometimes your peers have great tips you might not have thought of.

3. Avoid Retakes at All Costs

Retakes may seem like a second chance, but they come at a heavy price—time. Juggling new assignments with old ones is a recipe for stress and burnout. If you focus and stay organized, you can avoid retakes altogether. Plan your study schedule carefully, so you aren't cramming everything into the last minute.

4. Don't Be Afraid of the Easy Stuff

You'll come across subjects that seem difficult at first, but don't make them harder than they are. Sometimes, we overthink the easy courses, believing they should be more challenging. Keep things simple where you can and focus your energy on the truly tough subjects. Don't turn a molehill into a mountain!

5. Take Studying Seriously—Especially for Exams

This one may sound obvious, but skipping exams or not taking your study time seriously can seriously set you back. It's not just about passing; it's about building knowledge for your future career (like a friend of mine put it, make sure your company isn't hiring you based on a ChatGPT education(lol)). Make sure you spend enough time at home studying—sometimes, school won't give you all the learning time you need, and the extra hours make all the difference.

By embracing these small but meaningful changes, you can set yourself up for a more enjoyable, fulfilling freshman year. Stay curious, stay connected, and remember—asking for help is a strength, not a weakness.

Sincerely, A Wiser You
